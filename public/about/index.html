April 5, 2026

Who am I?#

I’m a 30-year-old from Latin America. A few months ago I was laid off from my job. I have about 18 months of runway in savings; my plan is to use this time to prepare for what I actually want to do: go back to college and pursue a STEM degree.

What’s the plan?#

The goal is to enroll in a public university next year to study either mathematics or physics (I haven’t decided yet, and I’m giving myself the space to figure that out). Fortunately, tuition at public universities here is free, so my main challenge is financial sustainability.

For this year, I intend to use this time to polish my math skills and learn in advance some of the subjects I know I will face. It’s been a while since I’ve been in any kind of academic environment, so I’m using this time to rebuild my foundations and get myself ready.

I don’t plan on going back to full-time work, but I’ll likely pick up freelance or part-time work along the way to extend my runway.

What am I studying?#

Right now, my focus is math. I’d estimate I’m roughly at the level of a Calculus I course. My primary resource is Math Academy, and I’ll go into more detail about my study methods and tools in future posts.

What is this blog?#

This is my accountability system. Every Sunday, I’ll post a weekly progress report covering two things:

Studies: what I worked on, what I learned, what I struggled with.

Finances: how I’m managing my runway.

The reason I’m doing this publicly is that writing things down forces me to reflect, and maybe knowing someone might read it helps keep me from slacking off.

If any of this resonates with you, or if you’re on a similar path, feel free to reach out on X (@HypatiaLog).
